**Result: 5 vs 0 - Agent Consensus Dominates**

All five research agents provide consistent, data-backed analysis that directly contradicts the GNN's "Insufficient Data" assessment. The agents collectively demonstrate:

1. **Clear Historical & Projected Trends**: Cryptojacking shows distinct phases of explosive growth (2019-2021), sharp decline (2021-2023), and adaptive resurgence (2024-2027).

2. **Evolution in Tactics**: Shift from noisy browser-based attacks to stealthy cloud/IoT targeting, fileless techniques, and supply chain compromises.

3. **Persistent Economic Impact**: Continuous financial drain through resource theft, with cloud environments being particularly vulnerable.

4. **Geopolitical Dimensions**: Use by state actors for revenue generation and as a low-risk attack vector.

**Strategic Recommendation**:  
Organizations should treat cryptojacking as a **persistent, evolving threat** requiring continuous monitoring rather than periodic assessment. Defensive strategies must evolve beyond the GNN-suggested solutions to include:

- **Behavioral analytics** and anomaly detection for resource consumption
- **Cloud-specific protections** (CSPM, workload security, cost monitoring)
- **Supply chain security** and software composition analysis
- **Integration with broader CTEM** (Continuous Threat Exposure Management) programs

The unanimous agent consensus indicates that cryptojacking has matured into an endemic threat that will persist as long as cryptocurrency remains valuable and computing resources are inadequately protected. Organizations should allocate resources accordingly, with particular focus on cloud and hybrid environments where the next wave of attacks is projected to concentrate.
